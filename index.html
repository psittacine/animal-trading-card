Northern Bald Ibis

Geronticus eremita

Critically Endangered: Wild population of <400 individuals, with population trend decreasing. Conservation programs in zoos, called Species Survival PlanÂ® Programs, are critical to the survival of this species.

  • Other Names: Waldrapp Ibis, Hermit Ibis
  • Distribution: Morocco; Syria (possibly regionally extinct); Turkey (reintroduced).
  • Habitat: Arid/semi-arid plains & plateaux with rocky escarpments; cultivated fields; nests/roosts in cliffs, adjacent to watercourses.
  • Diet: Invertebrates; small reptiles; occasionally small mammals, nestling birds & vegetable matter.
  • Lifespan: Up to 25 years.
  • Size: 75cm; wingspan 130cm; 1.2kg

Sometimes mistaken for a vulture due to its bald head & black feathers. This non-wading species of ibis uses its long bill to poke into rocky crevices, tufts of vegetation, or into soft ground when foraging for food.
